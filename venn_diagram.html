u − (v − u) == u

Set Theory · Venn Diagram Walkthrough

Step 01 Start: u and v
u = {1,2,3}  |  v = {2,3,4}

Two sets u and v may overlap partially, fully, or not at all.

Step 02 v − u (only-v region)
v − u = {4}

v − u contains only elements that are in v but NOT in u. This region never touches u.

Step 03 u − (v−u) = u ✓
u − (v−u) = {1,2,3} = u

Since v−u is always outside u, subtracting it from u removes nothing. u stays unchanged.

⟶ Why this is always true

u − (v − u) ≡ u

The set v − u is defined as elements in v that are not in u.
This means v − u and u are always disjoint — they share no elements.

Subtracting a disjoint set from u can never remove anything from u.
Therefore the equation holds for any possible u and v.

u (always preserved)
v − u (always outside u)
result = u ✓
